MANIFESTO
01
The body is a horror story the mind keeps trying to edit.

These drawings are confessions. Each line is a decision to stay in the discomfort, to not look away from the thing that lives in the corner of the eye.

Horror is not a genre here. It is a methodology. The surreal is not decoration. It is the only honest language for what the body knows and the mind cannot name.

Rooted in Assam. Editor-in-Chief of as flowers literary magazine. Poet. Visual artist. Someone who believes queer literature and horror manga are not as far apart as people think.

THE ARTIST

ASHIKUR
RAHMAN

ASSAM, INDIA / 2025

Ashikur is a poet, visual artist, and editor from Assam, India. His work moves between horror manga darkness and soft, intimate warmth. Sometimes in the same piece.

The drawings are not illustrations. They are confessions made in ink. Each line is a decision to stay in the discomfort.

Editor-in-Chief of as flowers literary magazine. Rooted in Assamese identity, queer literature, and a horror manga aesthetic that refuses to be categorized. The work carries that geography even when it doesn't announce it.

HOW IT HAPPENS

THE
PROCESS

01
👁
THE HAUNTING

It starts with an image that won't leave. Not a concept, a feeling. Something seen in the peripheral vision of the mind.

02
✒
THE MARK

Ink first. Always ink. The irreversibility is the point. Digital tools come later, but the first mark must be committed, must carry the weight of something that cannot be undone.

03
🩸
THE DARKNESS

The shadows are built slowly. Layer by layer. Horror is not about the monster. It is about the space where the monster might be.

04
🕯
THE TENDERNESS

Then the light. Not to comfort, to complicate. The warmth in a horror piece is not relief. It is the thing that makes the horror unbearable.
